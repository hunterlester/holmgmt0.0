Leverage Herd Behavior
(Put Your Animals to Work)

  • The Bottom Line
    • Your cash profits are dependent upon productive energy input
    • Get the most free energy input you can from solar energy, absorbed by dense plant life
    • Use the free labor of herd animals to fertilize your land, stimulate new plant growth, and to turn that plant life into valueable livestock products
  • What's Changing?
    • Manage animals as dense herd, not as individual browsers
    • What will change is the amount of time the herd spends in a given paddock or area
    • No need to spend extra money to increase herd size
    • The denser herd will be moved frequently once the ground is effectively grazed and impacted
    • Cost-effective capital investment in electric fencing and irrigation solutions
  • How It Works
    • Your ruminant herd will:
      • Fertilize your land with urine and manure, saving you money on imported fertilizer inputs
      • Trample down grass litter and biologically decompose plants, returning minerals to the soil to increase grassland productivity
      • Properly graze plants at the right time, cleaning up old plant matter and stimulating new growth
      • Break-up bare and capped soil to allow for new and denser plant growth
        • Unmanaged individual browsing leads to overresting and overgrazing
        • Overresting and overgrazing lead to decreased profits and drought conditions
  • Drought Mitigation
    • Using your herd to increase plant density per square yard:
      • Creates more dynamic soil life and root systems that hold more moisture instead of allowing run-off
      • Allows land to stay greener and to capture more moisture, leveraging available rain and irrigation without running up your water bill
      • Managing your land to create an effective water cycle will help to restore your acquifers. Yes.
