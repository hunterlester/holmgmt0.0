Plan to Pay Your Business First
(Revenues Are Not Cash Profit)

  • Recap: Saving and Making More Money
    • Energy and Feed:
      Capturing more free energy with dense plant life; your free animal forage
    • Labor:
      Using the free labor of your animal herd to maintain your land
    • Water:
      Your land can now capture more water and will be able to restore acquifers
    • Marketing:
      You're livestock products are now easy to differentiate, commanding higher pricing than commodity markets
    • The primary theme here is expending the least cost inputs to fetch the most profits
      • A seemingly obvious concept, however, agricultural production tends to miss that mark, mostly to line the pockets of a few major vendors instead of increasing profits for farmers
  • Now: Planning for Profits
    • Business planning does not account for human nature
    • We tend to increase expenses with increased income
    • Planning business expenses around projected revenue will produce the same results
    • Determine your desired profit margin for financial sustainability and growth
    • That profit margin must be paid to your business
    • Your business expenses are not allowed to reduce profit margin
    • Think about your business as a vendor. A vendor that must be paid first before all other vendor and expenses
