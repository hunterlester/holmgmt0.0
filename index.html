Leverage Herd Behavior
(Put Your Animals to Work)

  • The bottom line:
    1. Your cash profits are dependent upon energy input
    2. Get the most free energy input you can from solar energy, absorbed by dense plant life
    3. Use the free labor of herd animals to fertilize your land, stimulate new plant growth, and to turn that plant life into valueable livestock products
  • No need to spend extra money to increase herd size
  • Manage animals as dense herd, not as individual browsers
  • What will change is the amount of time the herd spends in a given paddock or area
  • The denser herd will be moved frequently once the ground is effectively grazed and impacted
  • Unmanaged individual browsing leads to overresting and overgrazing
  • Overresting and overgrazing lead to decreased profits and drought conditions
  • Ruminant animals, managed to behave as a herd:
    1. Fertilize your land with urine and manure, saving you money on imported fertilizer inputs
    2. Trample down grass litter and biologically decompose plants, returning minerals to the soil to increase grassland productivity
    3. Properly graze plants at the right time, cleaning up old plant matter and stimulating new growth
    4. Break-up bare and capped soil to allow for new and denser plant growth
  • Increased Plant Density per Square Yard:
    1. Creates more dynamic soil life and root systems that hold more moisture instead of allowing run-off
    2. Land stays greener and holds moisture leveraging available rain and irrigation without running up your water bill
    3. Enough land that's made to be dense and completely covered can reverse this drought and restore your acquifers. Yes.

Plan to Pay Your Business First
(Revenues Are Not Cash Profit)

  • Recap of how you're making and saving more money:
    1. Energy and Feed:
      Capturing more free energy with dense plant life; your free animal forage
    2. Labor:
      Using the free labor of your animal herd to maintain your land
    3. Water:
      Your land can now capture more water and will be able to restore acquifers
    4. Marketing:
      You're livestock products are now easy to differentiate, commanding higher pricing than commodity markets
    5. The primary theme here is expending the least cost inputs to fetch the most profits
      1. A seemingly obvious concept, however, agricultural production tends to miss that mark, mostly to line the pockets of a few major vendors instead of increasing profits for farmers
  • Now: Planning for Profits
    1. Accounting does not account for human nature
    2. We tend to increase expenses with increased income
    3. Planning business expenses around projected revenue will produce the same results
    4. Determine your desired profit margin for financial sustainability and growth
    5. That profit margin must be paid to your business
    6. Your business expenses are not allowed to reduce profit margin
    7. Think about your business as a vendor. A vendor that must be paid first before all other vendor and expenses
